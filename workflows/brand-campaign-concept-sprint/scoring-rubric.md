# Scoring rubric — brand campaign concept sprint

Score each criterion 1–5. Every score must cite evidence from the territory package, the brief, or the brand guidelines. A 3 means adequate; reserve 5 for territories that make the criterion a strength.

Claims and brand violations do not score low — they disqualify, with the offending line quoted.

| Criterion | Weight | What a 5 looks like |
|---|---|---|
| Brief fit | 3 | The territory answers the business objective and is built on the audience insight, not on what entertained the team |
| Audience resonance | 3 | The idea lands with the audience described in the brief, in their language, at their moment of need |
| Brand fit | 2 | Sounds and looks like this brand at its best; uses the voice rules as fuel rather than a constraint to escape |
| Distinctiveness | 2 | Would be recognized as this campaign with the logo removed; not the category's default move |
| Channel stretch | 1 | The idea has obvious, specific expressions in every in-scope channel, not just the hero film |
| Risk honesty | 1 | The package names what the territory risks and sacrifices, and where it could fail |

## Disqualifying checks (pass/fail)

- Uses a forbidden or unsubstantiated claim, or paraphrases an approved claim
- Violates a mandatory (legal line, lockup usage, channel scope)
- Breaks a "things this brand never does" rule without flagging it as a deliberate, named risk
