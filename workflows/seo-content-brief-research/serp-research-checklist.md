# SERP research checklist

What the serp-researcher agent records for each ranking page. Useful as a manual checklist too, when a strategist wants to spot-check the agent's notes.

## Per ranking page

- [ ] URL and publisher
- [ ] Page type: guide, comparison, listicle, documentation, tool/calculator, forum thread, video page
- [ ] Heading structure (H2/H3 outline, abbreviated)
- [ ] Approximate depth: word range, number of sections, level of detail
- [ ] Notable elements: tables, pricing, templates, checklists, calculators, screenshots, videos, FAQs
- [ ] Freshness signals: visible publish/update dates, version numbers, references to current releases
- [ ] Author and expertise signals: named author, credentials, first-party data or experience
- [ ] What the page does well for this searcher
- [ ] What the page misses or answers badly

## Across the SERP

- [ ] What every ranking page shares (structure, format, elements)
- [ ] Where the pages differ
- [ ] Questions none of them answer
- [ ] Whether the SERP is fresh or visibly outdated
- [ ] Any disagreement between the SERP's format and the apparent search intent

## Reminders

- Findings are observational: what ranking pages contain, not why they rank.
- No invented statistics or dates; cite only what you saw, with URLs.
- Never recommend keyword stuffing, doorway pages, or content aimed at search engines rather than readers.
