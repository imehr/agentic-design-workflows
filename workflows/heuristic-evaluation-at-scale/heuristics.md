## Nielsen's 10 (NN-01 to NN-10)

- **NN-01 Visibility of system status** — the UI keeps users informed about what is happening through timely feedback.
- **NN-02 Match between system and the real world** — words and concepts familiar to the user, in a natural order.
- **NN-03 User control and freedom** — clearly marked exits, undo, and redo.
- **NN-04 Consistency and standards** — the same words and patterns mean the same thing everywhere.
- **NN-05 Error prevention** — the design prevents problems before they happen.
- **NN-06 Recognition rather than recall** — options visible, instructions retrievable, nothing memorized between steps.
- **NN-07 Flexibility and efficiency of use** — accelerators for experienced users without confusing new ones.
- **NN-08 Aesthetic and minimalist design** — no irrelevant or rarely needed information competing for attention.
- **NN-09 Help users recognize, diagnose, and recover from errors** — plain-language errors that name a way out.
- **NN-10 Help and documentation** — easy to search, focused on the user's task, listing concrete steps.

## Project heuristics (replace with your own, 5–8 maximum)

- **PRJ-01** The primary object of the product (balance, board, document) is reachable within one tap/click from launch and never behind promotional content.
- **PRJ-02** Any irreversible or money-moving action shows what will happen on a confirmation step before commit.
- **PRJ-03** Destructive actions require explicit confirmation that names the consequence.
- **PRJ-04** Costs, fees, or limits are shown before the user commits, not in the receipt or after the fact.
- **PRJ-05** Every error state names the next action the user can take, in the user's language not the system's.
