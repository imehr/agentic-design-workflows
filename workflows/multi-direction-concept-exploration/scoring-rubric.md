# Scoring rubric

Score each criterion 1–5. Every score must cite evidence from the direction package or the
brief. A 3 means adequate; reserve 5 for directions that make the criterion a strength.
Constraint violations (legal, accessibility, platform) are disqualifying, not a low score.

| Criterion | Weight | What a 5 looks like |
|---|---|---|
| Job completion | 3 | The user reaches the brief's defined outcome with the fewest unforced decisions |
| Constraint fit | 3 | No conflicts with legal, accessibility, or platform constraints |
| Clarity of hierarchy | 2 | Each screen has one obvious primary action and the structure explains itself |
| Effort to ship | 2 | Reuses existing components and patterns; estimate S or M with believable reasoning |
| Differentiation | 1 | The direction is structurally distinct from the current product and the other stances |
| Risk honesty | 1 | The package names what the direction sacrifices and where it could fail |

Weighted total = sum of (score × weight). Report both the raw scores and the weighted total.
