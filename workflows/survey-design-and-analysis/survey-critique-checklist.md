# Survey critique checklist

The checklist the survey-critic agent works from. Useful for human review too —
print it next to the draft and walk question by question.

## Wording

- [ ] No leading or loaded wording (the question does not suggest its answer).
- [ ] No double-barreled items (one question, one thing).
- [ ] No jargon, internal feature names, or abbreviations the respondent may not know.
- [ ] No hypotheticals where a question about actual recent behavior would do.
- [ ] Recall windows are realistic ("in the last 30 days", not "typically").

## Answer options

- [ ] Options are exhaustive — the most common real situations all have a home.
- [ ] Options are mutually exclusive (or the question allows multiple selection).
- [ ] Scales are balanced, with labeled endpoints and a consistent direction.
- [ ] A genuine neutral and a not-applicable / don't-know escape exist where honesty needs them.
- [ ] No agree/disagree stacking where construct-specific wording would be clearer.

## Structure and order

- [ ] Every question maps to a construct in the brief; no orphan questions.
- [ ] Every construct in the brief has at least one question.
- [ ] Earlier questions do not prime later ones (especially brand, price, and satisfaction items).
- [ ] Sensitive and demographic questions come late.
- [ ] The survey length matches the incentive and the audience's patience.

## Before launch

- [ ] Persona read-aloud pass done (rushed mobile respondent, non-native speaker, power user, churned user).
- [ ] Piloted with at least five real people.
- [ ] The segments you will cut by are captured as columns, not inferred later.
- [ ] You know what decision the results will inform — and what answer would change it.
